## What we demoed

The talk covered two things.

First, we walked through the current production Enscribe app and why contract naming matters. Smart contracts are still identified by hexadecimal addresses, which are hard for humans to verify and easy to spoof. Assigning ENS names to contracts gives users a readable identifier that reflects what the contract is and who published it, and it removes a common class of address poisoning attacks at signing time.

Second, we demoed our new platform for ENS management, built for teams and protocols that need to manage onchain identity across contracts, wallets, multisigs, and agents. This is the direction we wrote about in our recent post on [moving from contract naming to identity infrastructure](/blog/from-contract-naming-to-identity-infrastructure). The new platform adds shared workspaces, record and metadata management, Safe and multisig support, and API and CLI access for deployment pipelines and agent workflows.
